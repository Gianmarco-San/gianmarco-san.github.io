DeepFake Detection Exploiting Self-Attention Maps


Research focuses on two-step DeepFake detection:

  • Extracting Self-Attention heatmaps
  • Training a convolutional autoencoder

The Vision Transformer (ViT), pre-trained on face recognition - starting from Face-Transformer -, captures facial features and extracts visual attention maps.
Images are split into multiple patches as input-tokens to the transformer encoder.

These are used to train the convolutional autoencoder - starting from the one developed in WildCapture - only on real images, enhancing out-of-distribution (OOD) detection by expecting higher reconstruction errors for manipulated images.

Tested on 5 different forgeries, the method shows an AUROC mean value of 0.60, demonstrating potential for transfer learning and generalizability among potential new forgeries.

CIFAR-10 Images Classifier


In this project I used CNNs to recognize color images from the CIFAR-10 Dataset.

Labels are integers, values are float and every image is made by 3 channels (R,G,B) with 32 x 32 pixels.

Here, an example of an image from the dataset, an horse.
The CIFAR-10 dataset consists of 60000 32x32 colour images in 10 classes, with 6000 images per class.

Dataset is splitted into train, validation and test sets as follow:
  • Elements in Train set: 48000
  • Elements in Valid set: 2000
  • Elements in Test set: 10000
First attempt to images-recogntion with MultiLayerPerceptron (MLP), as a baseline.

This simple MLP with decreasing size of fully connected hidden layers is the best trade-off between complexity and performace found in test.

Learning rate varies over the epochs, starting with 0.1 and gradually decreasing to about 0.001 at epoch 20
The resulting confusion matrix is shown, with accuracy on test set equal to 53.4%.

The most misclassified by the model is Cat (accuracy of 33.3%) along with Dog and similarly, Deer with Bird.

While the best classified is Automobile with 66.4%.

Finally, there is an imperfect simmetry among classes, e.g. Cat vs Dog and Dog vs Cat.
Second model with convolutionalNerualNetwork (CNN) with two convolutional layers and a max pooling.

The learning rate varies in the same way as MLP.
Despite a longer training time, the CNN model performed much better than the MLP with a accuracy of 70,4% vs 53,7% on Test set.

Both models have similar tendency to predict worse some classes, such as Cat.

Fitness classes. Do you book and go?


In this Project I try to predict whether a member will attend a class or not, ones booked.
You'll find:
  • EDA.
  • Data management.
  • Models and their performance.
Considering graphs, there are some tendencies:
  • Weight vs attendance goes down.
  • Attendance grows with months as member.
This can lead to operative choice of make the booking of loyal users (the ones with more months as member) affordable.
As expected, there is inverse correlation between mean values of Weight and mean values of Months as member.
Confusion Matrix with best classifier:
  • LogisticRegression(random_state=23)
  • Mean accuracy score: 0.7583
By changing the proporions of train and test sets I can see that the accuracy is quite stable.

There is a tendency for outcome to be predicted "0".
Considering the unbalanced dataset, where much more records have values "0" as attendance, somehow models tend to learn to predict more often labels of lower value.

How a 30 leaf nodes equipped Decision Tree Classifier looks like.

Design of a two-stage reduction gearbox for general applications


The goal was to design a two-stage parallel-axis pendular gearbox.

Shafts and gears were dimensioned based on key design specifications, starting with a conservative approach and refining iteratively to minimize oversizing.


Bearings were selected with attention to assembly challenges, and the gearbox was completed with the most suitable mechanical components, aiming for a practical design.

In this image, you can see a section of the assembly drawing of the gearbox.

The design of one of the drive shafts.
