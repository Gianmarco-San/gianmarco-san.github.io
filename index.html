DeepFake Detection Exploiting Self-Attention Maps


Worked on two-step DeepFake detection:
  • Extracting Self-Attention heatmaps
  • Training a convolutional autoencoder

Worked on two-step DeepFake detection:
  • Extracting Self-Attention heatmaps
  • Training a convolutional autoencoder

The Vision Transformer (ViT), pre-trained on face recognition - starting from Face-Transformer -, captures facial features and extracts visual attention maps.
Images are split into multiple patches as input-tokens to the transformer encoder.

These are used to train the convolutional autoencoder - starting from the one developed in WildCapture - only on real images, enhancing out-of-distribution (OOD) detection by expecting higher reconstruction errors for manipulated images.

Tested on 5 different forgeries, the method shows an AUROC mean value of 0.60, demonstrating potential for transfer learning and generalizability among potential new forgeries.

In this project I used CNNs to recognize color images from the CIFAR-10 Dataset.

Labels are integers, values are float and every image is made by 3 channels (R,G,B) with 32 x 32 pixels.

Here, an example of an image from the dataset, an horse.
The CIFAR-10 dataset consists of 60000 32x32 colour images in 10 classes, with 6000 images per class.

Dataset is splitted into train, validation and test sets as follow:
  • Elements in Train set: 48000
  • Elements in Valid set: 2000
  • Elements in Test set: 10000
First attempt to images-recogntion with MultiLayerPerceptron (MLP), as a baseline.

Second model with convolutionalNerualNetwork (CNN) with two convolutional layers and a max pooling.

Learning rate varies over the epochs, starting with 0.1 and gradually decreasing to about 0.001 at epoch 20.
Despite a longer training time, the CNN model performed much better than the MLP with a accuracy of 70,4% vs 53,7% on Test set.

Both models have similar tendency to predict worse some classes, such as Cat.

Images are related to CNN.

To name some projects and responsibilities:

  • I developed a sensor-to-cloud temperature control system optimizing energy efficiency and safety in the factory through real-time data processing. You can find some details below.
  • I built and maintained services, such as periodic reports via proprietary cloud-based web API.


Other tasks included:

In the temperature control system project, I used E+E Sensors to measure the temperature in each of the 9 main columns of the factory, on 2 or 3 levels.

All data-points have been processed and displayed via cloud on a web interface to show real-time behavior of the system.

I developed the control logic on the PLC to activate the heating system when a weighted temperature measurement, based on all available data-points, falls below a threshold.
Similarly, when the temperature exceeds the threshold, the cooling system activates.

Optimized Infrastructure Design for Wireless Charging of Electric Vehicles


In this work I focused on optimizing the infrastructure layout for dynamic and static wireless charging systems for industrial electric vehicles, developed by Enermove.

The Wireless Power Transfer (WPT) system enables contactless charging for forklifts and other logistics vehicles, eliminating the need for dedicated charging stations and minimizing downtime.

The research develops a mathematical model to determine the optimal placement and number of transmitting coils to balance charging efficiency, operational logistics, and cost minimization.

The Wireless Power Transfer (WPT) system enables contactless charging for forklifts and other logistics vehicles, eliminating the need for dedicated charging stations and minimizing downtime.

The research develops a mathematical model to determine the optimal placement and number of transmitting coils to balance charging efficiency, operational logistics, and cost minimization.

The methodology involves discretizing the warehouse layout into computational cells, analyzing vehicle movement patterns, charging time, and energy consumption.

The model integrates genetic algorithms in MATLAB to optimize the coil placement based on vehicle traffic density, energy demands and predict high-priority charging zones.
The energy, economic, and operational impact of different charging configurations are considered.

Additionally, cost-benefit analysis confirms that dynamic charging reduces overall infrastructure costs by minimizing the need for stationary charging points.

Simulation results demonstrate that strategic coil placement significantly improves battery State of Charge (SoC) during operations.
The proposed optimized WPT infrastructure presents a scalable and adaptable solution for smart logistics and industrial automation.

Future developments may explore its integration with renewable energy sources, AI-driven traffic management, and broader applications in autonomous vehicle fleets.

Fluid in Conduit Heat Exchange Numerical Modeling


We worked on the finite element numerical modeling of heat exchange in a flue gas conduit, simulating convective-diffusive thermal transfer.

Based on a previously designed air-based residential boiler, you can see the project below.

We worked on the finite element numerical modeling of heat exchange in a flue gas conduit, simulating convective-diffusive thermal transfer.

Based on a previously designed air-based residential boiler, you can see the project below.

We examated how heat dissipates from the combustion gases flowing through the chimney, both inside and outside the building, quantifying temperature distribution and energy recovery.

The governing equation accounts for convective heat transfer within the flue gases and conductive transfer through the metal walls, using a cylindrical coordinate system for computational efficiency.

The model incorporates boundary conditions for internal and external environments, including Dirichlet, Neumann, and Robin conditions, ensuring realistic heat exchange representation.
We applied finite element discretization to solve the stationary and transient heat transfer problem.

The mesh resolution was optimized to balance computational cost and accuracy, using Gaussian elimination for matrix resolution.

The transient simulation models the system’s thermal evolution, validating that steady-state conditions match real operational scenarios.
The sensitivity analysis was conducted by varying the gas velocity, demonstrating its impact on heat dissipation and flue gas temperature at the outlet.

Results confirmed that lower velocities increase heat loss, reducing the final temperature of exhaust gases.

Design and optimized a residential air-based boiler using methane combustion.

The system features two concentric chambers:
  • An inner combustion chamber
  • An outer airflow chamber for heat distribution
Two fans regulate combustion air intake and heated air circulation, while an integrated chimney enhances energy recovery from flue gases.

The thermodynamic analysis includes stoichiometric combustion modeling, temperature calculations, and efficiency evaluation.

The heat exchanger, sized via LMTD methodology, was analyzed in finned and non-finned configurations to maximize heat transfer.

Radiation effects and insulation were also considered to minimize thermal losses.
The ventilation system was dimensioned based on pressure drop calculations and flow balancing, ensuring optimal air distribution.

Fan power requirements were determined using fluid dynamics principles, accounting for concentrated and distributed pressure losses.

An exergy analysis quantified system irreversibilities and efficiency, optimizing heat recovery through the chimney.

Mixed Alloy Chip Extrusion


I've conducted this research at the Institut für Umformtechnik und Leichtbau (IUL), TU Dortmund, combining experiments and FEM simulations.

The direct recycling of aluminum machining chips using hot extrusion (Chip Extrusion), avoids re-melting to produce high-quality profiles.

The research explores the feasibility of extruding mixed alloy chips of varying shapes and sizes, optimizing processing parameters to achieve structural and aesthetic properties comparable to standard cast billets and assessing the feasibility of the process at an industrial level.

The process involves chip classification, cleaning, and cold compaction into billets before undergoing hot extrusion using porthole and flat-face dies.


Tensile, hardness, and microstructural analyses assess the bonding quality between chips, identifying key factors like oxide layer disruption and strain levels.

Chip-based extrudates can achieve comparable mechanical properties to cast billets, with some cases exhibiting higher strength due to grain refinement and oxide layer effects.
FEM simulations using HyperMesh, HyperExtrude and MATLAB validate the experimental findings, modeling stress, strain, and temperature distributions during extrusion.

The welding line criteria has been applied to predict chip bonding and optimize process parameters.

Results show that higher extrusion ratios and optimized die designs significantly improve mechanical performance and surface quality.
The research demonstrates an energy-efficient recycling alternative with 3-4% additional energy savings over conventional secondary aluminum re-melting.

While challenges remain in surface quality and porosity, as well as in the standardization of the process.

The findings suggest a viable path for industrial-scale adoption of chip extrusion in sustainable metal manufacturing.
